SOFTWARE DEVELOPER

General Info:

Job Description:

Research, design, and develop computer and network software or specialized utility programs. Analyze user needs and develop software solutions, applying principles and techniques of computer science, engineering, and mathematical analysis. Update software or enhance existing software capabilities. May work with computer hardware engineers to integrate hardware and software systems, and develop specifications and performance requirements. May maintain databases within an application area, working individually or coordinating database development as part of a team.

Educational requirements:

Employers typically expect in-depth coding, testing, and development knowledge, which a bachelor's degree can deliver. Common majors include information technology, computer science, and software engineering. As technology advances and the field becomes more competitive, some software developers pursue master's degrees to stand out among the competition.

Skills/Experience:

Soft skills: Communication, Teamwork, Problem-Solving, and Tracking Issues Hard skills: Data Structures, Algorithms, Security, and Programming Languages

Safety Info:

Software development safety involves securing code against issues, and protecting personal health. You should take care of yourself due to the job itself being stressful, you should validate user inputs, manage dependencies, and automate security testing. Developers should also focus on keeping a work-life balance, as to not let their job take over their life.

Job Outlook:

Overall employment of software developers, quality assurance analysts, and testers is more than likely to grow 15% from 2024 to 2034, significantly faster than the average for all occupations. About 129,200 openings for software developers are projected each year, on average. Many of those openings are expected to result from the need to replace workers who transfer to different jobs or exit the labor force, such as to retire.

Trends:

AI:

Human/AI Collaboration:

In practice, organizations that empower their people with AI will outpace those who don’t, and employees who know how to work with AI will outperform those who do not. Most corporations have discovered that rather than working against AI, they should work with it to create the best environment.

Task Automation v.s. Human Value:

    AI:

  • Pros:
    • can identify bugs quicker
    • can generate code/predict future lines of code
  • Cons:
    • bad for the environment
    • takes jobs from people who need it
    • its a big investment

    Human:

  • Pros:
    • naturally learns from past mistakes
    • can generate code/predict future lines of code
    • less expensive
    • doesn't consume a massive amount of resources
  • Cons:
    • isn’t as fast or effective for smaller tasks
    • can leave the job at any point or pursue another interest
    • prone to messing up
